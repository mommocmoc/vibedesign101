What we did

The Process

1

Find your vibe

Rather than picking from a pre-made set, each person surfaced the aesthetic they gravitate toward — the single biggest predictor of output quality.

2

Generate with Stitch · 2 rounds

Round one used text only (Idea · Contents · Theme); round two added reference images + a DESIGN.md — making the power of input context tangible.

3

Reflect together

We talked through what the agent did well, where it struggled and why, and what a designer's role becomes next.

03 / 08
What we noticed

Key Insights

Text alone rarely lands it

Driving generation with text prompts only kept drifting from intent. The limiting factor wasn't prompt cleverness — it was the thinness of the input context we gave.

References + a DESIGN.md close the gap

Pairing visual references with a structured DESIGN.md moved outputs noticeably closer to intent — and made clear which of mood, layout, type and color to spell out.

Strengths and blind spots are uneven

It handled complex composition with ease yet stumbled on trivial asks like a solid-color background. Always review outputs critically.

One thing to remember

Key Takeaway

H

Harness Engineering

In the age of AI agents, input context is everything. Write down the information and steps your design process needs — that becomes your asset for working with an agent.

A

AI Agent = Quick Refresher

A reliable partner that spins up many drafts fast and powers through repetitive edits in a single sweep.
